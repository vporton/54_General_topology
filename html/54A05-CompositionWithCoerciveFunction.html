composition with coercive function


Theorem 1.

Suppose X,Y,Z are topological spaces, f:X→Y is a bijective proper map, and g:Y→Z is a coercive map. Then g∘f:X→Z is a coercive map.

Proof.

Let J⊆Z be a compact set. As g is coercive, there is a compact set K⊆Y such that

g⁢(Y∖K)⊆Z∖J.

Let I=f-1⁢(K), and since f is a proper map I is compact. Thus

(g∘f)⁢(X∖I)=g⁢(Y∖K)⊆Z∖J

and g∘f is coercive. ∎
