Theorem 1.

[1] Every second countable space is separable.

Proof.

Let X be a second countable space and let ℬ be a countable base. For every non-empty set B in ℬ, choose a point xB∈B. The set A of all such points xB is clearly countable and it’s also dense since any open set intersects it and thus the whole space is the closure of A. That is, A is a countably dense subset of X. Therefore, X is separable. ∎
