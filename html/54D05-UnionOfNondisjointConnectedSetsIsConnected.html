union of non-disjoint connected sets is connected


Theorem 1.

Suppose A,B are connected sets in a topological space X. If A,B are not disjoint, then A∪B is connected.

Proof.

By assumption, we have two implications. First, if U,V are open in A and U∪V=A, then U∩V≠∅. Second, if U,V are open in B and U∪V=B, then U∩V≠∅. To prove that A∪B is connected, suppose U,V are open in A∪B and U∪V=A∪B. Then

U∪V = ((U∪V)∩A)∪((U∪V)∩B)
= (U∩A)∪(V∩A)∪(U∩B)∪(V∩B)

Let us show that U∩A and V∩A are open in A. To do this, we use this result (http://planetmath.org/SubspaceOfASubspace) and notation from that entry too. For example, as U∈τA∪B,X, U∩A∈τA,A∪B,X=τA,X, and so U∩A, V∩A are open in A. Since (U∩A)∪(V∩A)=A, it follows that

∅≠(U∩A)∩(V∩A)=(U∩V)∩A.

If U∩V=∅, then this is a contradition, so A∪B must be connected. ∎
