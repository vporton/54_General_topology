local homeomorphism


Definition. Let X and Y be topological spaces. Continuous map f:X→Y is said to be locally invertible in x∈X iff there exist open subsets U⊆X and V⊆Y such that x∈U, f⁢(x)∈V and the restriction

f:U→V

is a homeomorphism. If f is locally invertible in every point of X, then f is called a local homeomorphism.

Examples. Of course every homeomorphism is a local homeomorphism, but the converse is not true. For example, let f:ℂ→ℂ be an exponential function, i.e. f⁢(z)=ez. Then f is a local homeomorphism, but it is not a homeorphism (indeed, f⁢(z)=f⁢(z+2⁢π⁢i) for any z∈ℂ).

One of the most important theorem of differential calculus (i.e. inverse function theorem) states, that if f:M→N is a C1-map between C1-manifolds such that Tx⁢f:Tx⁢M→Tf⁢(x)⁢N is a linear isomorphism for a given x∈M, then f is locally invertible in x (in this case the local inverse is even a C1-map).
